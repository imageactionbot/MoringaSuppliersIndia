About the Plant

What is Moringa?

Moringa oleifera — the "Miracle Tree" native to India — is one of the most nutrient-dense plants on Earth.

🌱

Native to India

Cultivated for over 4,000 years across the Indian subcontinent. Every part of the tree — leaves, pods, seeds, flowers, roots, bark — has nutritional and medicinal value.

🌍

80% Global Share

India is the world's largest producer and exporter of Moringa, dominating global supply. Tamil Nadu, Maharashtra, and Andhra Pradesh are primary growing regions.

🔬

92+ Nutrients

Contains 9 essential amino acids, 27 vitamins, 46 antioxidants, and minerals including calcium, iron, magnesium, potassium, and zinc.

🌿

Whole Tree Use

Leaves to powder, seeds to oil, pods to drumsticks — nothing goes to waste. Moringa is truly a zero-waste crop with commercial value in every part.

Balanced View

Moringa: Pros & Cons

Everything you need to know before buying Moringa from India.

✓ Pros (Advantages)

  • Extremely high nutrient density — 7x more Vitamin C than oranges, 4x more calcium than milk
  • Rich in antioxidants that fight oxidative stress and inflammation
  • May help lower blood sugar and cholesterol naturally
  • Supports immune system, bone health, and lactation
  • Sustainable crop — drought-resistant, fast-growing, zero-waste plant
  • India supplies 80% of global demand — consistent availability
  • Multiple product forms (powder, oil, capsules, tea, seeds)
  • Certified organic options available with proper documentation

✗ Cons (Disadvantages)

  • Not all Moringa from India is certified organic — verify documents carefully
  • Can interact with blood thinners, diabetes medication, and thyroid drugs
  • Improper storage leads to nutrient loss and potential contamination
  • May cause digestive issues (nausea, gas) if consumed in large quantities
  • Roots contain alkaloids that can be toxic in high amounts
  • Pregnant women should consult doctor before consuming
  • Lower-quality products may contain fillers or adulteration
  • International shipping costs can be high for bulk orders

Farm to Export

How Moringa Reaches You: The 6-Stage Process

A transparent look at how high-quality Moringa moves from Indian farms to international buyers — every stage built around traceability, food safety and certification.

1

🌱 Cultivation

Moringa oleifera is grown on certified organic farms primarily across Tamil Nadu, Maharashtra, Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka and Rajasthan. Farms are typically managed without synthetic pesticides or fertilizers when targeting NPOP / USDA / EU Organic certification.

2

🌿 Harvesting

Leaves are hand-picked at peak nutrient density, typically 40–60 days after pruning cycles. Drumstick pods, seeds and bark are harvested separately by purpose (food, oil-extraction, or planting).

3

☀️ Shade Drying

Leaves are dried at low temperatures (typically below 50 °C) in clean, shaded environments to preserve chlorophyll, vitamins and antioxidants. Sun-drying or high-heat drying degrades nutrient content.

4

⚙️ Processing & Packaging

Cleaned, sieved and milled in food-grade facilities — usually GMP / ISO / FSSAI compliant. Packed in food-grade aluminium foil, kraft paper or bulk fiber drums depending on the buyer's spec.

5

🧪 Quality Testing

Each batch is tested at NABL-accredited labs for moisture, ash, microbial load, heavy metals (Pb, As, Hg, Cd), pesticide residues and aflatoxins. A batch-specific Certificate of Analysis (COA) is issued.

6

🚢 Export & Shipping

Phytosanitary certificate (PQIS), organic transaction certificate (TC) and shipping documents are issued. Goods leave via Chennai, Tuticorin, Mumbai or Mundra. Air freight: 5–7 days; sea freight: 25–40 days.

Planet & People

Sustainability & Ethical Sourcing

Moringa is one of the most sustainable cash crops on Earth — drought-resistant, fast-growing and almost entirely zero-waste. Buyers increasingly prefer Indian Moringa not just for quality but also for its environmental and social footprint.

💧

Low Water Footprint

Moringa thrives in semi-arid conditions with minimal irrigation — typically using a fraction of the water required by conventional cash crops.

🌳

Fast-Growing & Carbon Positive

Mature trees can be harvested within 6–8 months. Continuous foliage growth means active CO₂ absorption throughout the year.

♻️

Zero-Waste Crop

Every part of the tree has commercial value — leaves, pods, seeds, oil, bark, roots and even press-cake (used for water purification and animal feed).

👩‍🌾

Smallholder-Friendly

Indian Moringa cultivation involves thousands of smallholder and women farmers, making fair-trade and Fair-for-Life certifications increasingly common.

🍃

Pesticide-Free Potential

The plant's natural resilience reduces dependence on chemical inputs, making organic certification commercially feasible at scale.

🌾

Soil Regeneration

Moringa improves soil structure, supports nitrogen fixing intercropping and helps prevent desertification in degraded farmlands.

Trade Terminology

Moringa Trade Glossary

Key terms every international buyer should know before negotiating with an Indian Moringa supplier.

APEDA
Agricultural and Processed Food Products Export Development Authority of India — the body that registers and supports agri-product exporters.
NPOP
National Programme for Organic Production — India's official organic certification standard recognized by major export markets.
USDA Organic / NOP
United States organic standard. Required for products sold as "organic" in the US.
EU Organic (834/2007)
The European Union organic regulation. Required for the EU green-leaf organic logo.
COA
Certificate of Analysis — a batch-specific lab report covering moisture, ash, microbiology, heavy metals and pesticide residues.
NABL
National Accreditation Board for Testing and Calibration Laboratories. NABL-accredited labs issue internationally recognized test reports.
FSSAI
Food Safety and Standards Authority of India — issues licences for food businesses operating within India.
PQIS / Phytosanitary
Plant Quarantine Information System certificate confirming the consignment is free from regulated pests, required for plant-product export.
IEC
Importer Exporter Code issued by India's DGFT — mandatory for any company exporting from India.
FOB
Free On Board — buyer takes ownership and shipping cost responsibility once goods are loaded on the vessel at the Indian port.
CIF
Cost, Insurance and Freight — supplier covers cost up to destination port including marine insurance.
MOQ
Minimum Order Quantity — the smallest volume a supplier will accept per order.
LC (Letter of Credit)
A bank-issued payment instrument that protects both buyer and supplier in international trade.
TC (Transaction Certificate)
An organic-trade document proving a specific shipment meets the organic standard claimed by the supplier.
Aflatoxin
A naturally occurring mycotoxin tested for in dried plant products. Strict EU and US limits apply.
Heavy Metals Panel
Lab test for Lead (Pb), Arsenic (As), Mercury (Hg) and Cadmium (Cd). Critical for nutraceutical-grade Moringa.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Is all Indian Moringa organic?
No. Only certified organic Moringa is authentic. Always ask for NPOP, USDA, or EU Organic certificates from the supplier.
Which Indian state has the best Moringa suppliers?
Tamil Nadu is the largest producer (Madurai, Dindigul). Maharashtra (Solapur) is also an emerging hub.
What is the typical MOQ for Moringa powder?
Typically 25kg to 100kg for powder. Drumsticks may require 1 ton or more depending on the supplier.
Can buyers get samples before ordering?
Yes, most legitimate suppliers provide samples for a nominal fee plus international shipping cost.
How long does shipping take from India?
Air freight: 5-7 days. Sea freight: 25-40 days depending on destination port.
What payment methods do Indian suppliers accept?
Wire transfer (T/T), Letter of Credit (LC), or PayPal for samples.
Is Moringa safe for daily consumption?
Yes, in moderate amounts (1-2 teaspoons per day). Consult a doctor if pregnant, nursing, or on medication.
What is the difference between organic and conventional Moringa?
Organic Moringa is grown without synthetic pesticides or fertilizers and carries third-party certification.
