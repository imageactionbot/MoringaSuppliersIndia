About the Plant

What is Moringa?

Moringa oleifera — the "Miracle Tree" native to India — is one of the most nutrient-dense plants on Earth.

🌱

Native to India

Cultivated for over 4,000 years across the Indian subcontinent. Every part of the tree — leaves, pods, seeds, flowers, roots, bark — has nutritional and medicinal value.

🌍

80% Global Share

India is the world's largest producer and exporter of Moringa, dominating global supply. Tamil Nadu, Maharashtra, and Andhra Pradesh are primary growing regions.

🔬

92+ Nutrients

Contains 9 essential amino acids, 27 vitamins, 46 antioxidants, and minerals including calcium, iron, magnesium, potassium, and zinc.

🌿

Whole Tree Use

Leaves to powder, seeds to oil, pods to drumsticks — nothing goes to waste. Moringa is truly a zero-waste crop with commercial value in every part.

Balanced View

Moringa: Pros & Cons

Everything you need to know before buying Moringa from India.

✓ Pros (Advantages)

  • Extremely high nutrient density — 7x more Vitamin C than oranges, 4x more calcium than milk
  • Rich in antioxidants that fight oxidative stress and inflammation
  • May help lower blood sugar and cholesterol naturally
  • Supports immune system, bone health, and lactation
  • Sustainable crop — drought-resistant, fast-growing, zero-waste plant
  • India supplies 80% of global demand — consistent availability
  • Multiple product forms (powder, oil, capsules, tea, seeds)
  • Certified organic options available with proper documentation

✗ Cons (Disadvantages)

  • Not all Moringa from India is certified organic — verify documents carefully
  • Can interact with blood thinners, diabetes medication, and thyroid drugs
  • Improper storage leads to nutrient loss and potential contamination
  • May cause digestive issues (nausea, gas) if consumed in large quantities
  • Roots contain alkaloids that can be toxic in high amounts
  • Pregnant women should consult doctor before consuming
  • Lower-quality products may contain fillers or adulteration
  • International shipping costs can be high for bulk orders

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Is all Indian Moringa organic?
No. Only certified organic Moringa is authentic. Always ask for NPOP, USDA, or EU Organic certificates from the supplier.
Which Indian state has the best Moringa suppliers?
Tamil Nadu is the largest producer (Madurai, Dindigul). Maharashtra (Solapur) is also an emerging hub.
What is the typical MOQ for Moringa powder?
Typically 25kg to 100kg for powder. Drumsticks may require 1 ton or more depending on the supplier.
Can buyers get samples before ordering?
Yes, most legitimate suppliers provide samples for a nominal fee plus international shipping cost.
How long does shipping take from India?
Air freight: 5-7 days. Sea freight: 25-40 days depending on destination port.
What payment methods do Indian suppliers accept?
Wire transfer (T/T), Letter of Credit (LC), or PayPal for samples.
Is Moringa safe for daily consumption?
Yes, in moderate amounts (1-2 teaspoons per day). Consult a doctor if pregnant, nursing, or on medication.
What is the difference between organic and conventional Moringa?
Organic Moringa is grown without synthetic pesticides or fertilizers and carries third-party certification.
