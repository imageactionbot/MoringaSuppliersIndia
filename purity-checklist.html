Moringa leaf powder — retail purity checklist

MoringaSuppliersIndia.com · Educational use

  1. Colour & appearance Uniform vibrant green? Avoid large grey/brown clumps that may indicate oxidation, ash, or inconsistent drying.
  2. Aroma Fresh, grassy notes are typical. Musty, mould-like, or rancid odours warrant stopping and asking for documentation.
  3. Texture & mouthfeel (dry) Fine, sifted powder should flow evenly. Excessive grit may indicate soil, sand, or poor milling — escalate to a lab COA.
  4. Label & organic claims Match NPOP / USDA / EU organic marks to the certifier registry and certificate dates. Root powder and leaf powder are not interchangeable.
  5. Heavy metals & micro For anything beyond a pantry trial, request a batch COA (lead, cadmium, arsenic, mercury, aerobic plate count, yeast/mould as relevant).
